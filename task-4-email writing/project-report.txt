A situation of poor digital communication can occur in a college group project where one team member shares important instructions through a vague message like “submit the file by tomorrow.” Some members may assume different deadlines or formats, leading to incomplete or incorrect submissions. As a result, the group loses marks due to confusion rather than lack of effort. This problem arises from unclear messaging, lack of confirmation, and absence of proper coordination. To avoid such issues, the sender should provide complete details, including the exact deadline, file format, and submission method. Additionally, using structured communication, confirming understanding, and maintaining all updates on a common platform can ensure clarity and prevent misunderstandings among team members.
